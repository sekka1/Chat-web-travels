# Essential Packing Guide for International Travel

A comprehensive guide to help you pack efficiently for your travels.

## Universal Essentials

### Documents
- Passport (check expiration date - must be valid 6 months beyond travel)
- Visa (if required)
- Travel insurance documents
- Flight confirmations and itinerary
- Hotel reservations
- Driver's license (international if needed)
- Copies of important documents (keep separate from originals)
- Emergency contact information

### Money and Cards
- Credit cards (notify bank of travel plans)
- Debit card for ATM withdrawals
- Some local currency
- Money belt or hidden pouch
- Backup card kept separately

### Electronics
- Smartphone and charger
- Universal power adapter
- Portable power bank
- Camera (if not using phone)
- Headphones
- E-reader or tablet

### Health and Hygiene
- Prescription medications (in original containers)
- Basic first aid kit
- Hand sanitizer
- Sunscreen
- Insect repellent
- Any required vaccinations

## Climate-Specific Packing

### Tropical Destinations
- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Swimwear
- Sun hat
- Sunglasses
- Rain jacket or poncho
- Sandals and comfortable walking shoes

### Cold Weather Destinations
- Layering pieces (base, mid, outer layers)
- Warm coat or parka
- Gloves, hat, scarf
- Thermal underwear
- Waterproof boots
- Wool socks

### All-Season Essentials
- Versatile clothing that layers
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Light rain jacket
- One nice outfit for dining out

## Packing Tips

### General Rules
- Roll clothes instead of folding (saves space)
- Use packing cubes for organization
- Wear bulkiest items during travel
- Pack a change of clothes in carry-on
- Leave room for souvenirs

### Minimalist Approach
- Stick to a color scheme (mix and match easily)
- Plan to do laundry mid-trip
- Bring multi-purpose items
- Choose quick-dry fabrics
- Pack travel-sized toiletries

### Carry-On Essentials
- Medications
- Electronics
- Change of clothes
- Valuable items
- Travel documents
- Snacks
- Empty water bottle

## What NOT to Pack

- Expensive jewelry
- Too many "just in case" items
- Full-size toiletries (buy at destination or use travel sizes)
- Multiple heavy books (use e-reader)
- Too many shoes (2-3 pairs max)

## Special Considerations

### Business Travel
- Wrinkle-resistant business attire
- Portable steamer
- Business cards

### Adventure Travel
- Appropriate gear for activities
- Quick-dry clothing
- Sturdy hiking boots
- Dry bags for electronics

### Beach Destinations
- Multiple swimsuits (one dries while wearing the other)
- Beach bag
- Waterproof phone case
- Reef-safe sunscreen

## TSA/Security Guidelines

### Carry-On Liquids
- 3.4 oz (100ml) or less per item
- All items in one quart-sized clear bag
- One bag per passenger

### Prohibited Items (Carry-On)
- Sharp objects
- Sporting equipment
- Tools
- Firearms and ammunition (check for checked baggage rules)

## Sustainable Travel Packing

- Reusable water bottle
- Cloth shopping bag
- Bamboo utensils
- Solid toiletries (shampoo bars, solid soap)
- Reusable food containers

## Pre-Trip Checklist

One Week Before:
- [ ] Check passport expiration
- [ ] Notify banks of travel
- [ ] Arrange pet care/house sitting
- [ ] Hold mail or arrange forwarding
- [ ] Confirm all reservations

Day Before:
- [ ] Charge all electronics
- [ ] Print boarding passes
- [ ] Check weather at destination
- [ ] Pack medications in carry-on
- [ ] Set home security

Day of Travel:
- [ ] Check-in online
- [ ] Verify flight status
- [ ] Leave house keys with trusted person
- [ ] Adjust thermostat
- [ ] Lock all doors and windows
