# Mexico City Travel Guide

Mexico City (CDMX) is a vibrant megalopolis blending ancient Aztec heritage, Spanish colonial architecture, and modern cosmopolitan culture. As one of Latin America's largest cities, it offers world-class museums, incredible food, historic neighborhoods, and rich cultural experiences.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (March-May)**: Pleasant weather, ideal temperatures (60-75°F/15-24°C)
- **Fall (September-November)**: Post-rainy season, comfortable weather, Day of the Dead celebrations
- **Winter (December-February)**: Coolest months, clear skies, holiday festivities
- **Summer (June-August)**: Rainy season with afternoon showers, but still warm

## Top Neighborhoods & Districts

### Centro Histórico (Historic Center)
The heart of Mexico City with ancient ruins and colonial architecture:
- Zócalo (Main Square) - one of the largest city squares in the world
- Templo Mayor - Aztec temple ruins
- Palacio Nacional - murals by Diego Rivera
- Torre Latinoamericana - panoramic city views
- Metropolitan Cathedral

### Coyoacán
Charming colonial neighborhood with artistic heritage:
- Museo Frida Kahlo (Blue House) - former home of Frida Kahlo
- Museo Casa Estudio Diego Rivera y Frida Kahlo
- Coyoacán Market - local crafts and traditional food
- Jardín Centenario - central plaza with cafes
- Viveros de Coyoacán - peaceful park

### Roma & Condesa
Trendy neighborhoods with Art Nouveau architecture:
- Hip cafes, restaurants, and bars
- Vintage shops and boutiques
- Parks: Parque México and Parque España
- Street art and galleries
- Vibrant nightlife scene

### San Ángel
Upscale colonial area in southern Mexico City:
- Saturday art market (Bazar Sábado)
- Plaza San Jacinto
- Museo Casa del Risco
- University City (UNAM) - UNESCO World Heritage site

### Polanco & Chapultepec
Upscale area with world-class museums:
- Chapultepec Park - largest city park in Latin America
- National Museum of Anthropology
- Chapultepec Castle
- Museo Tamayo
- Luxury shopping on Avenida Presidente Masaryk

## Must-See Attractions

### Museums
- **National Museum of Anthropology** - world's finest collection of Mesoamerican art
- **Museo Frida Kahlo** - intimate look at Frida Kahlo's life and art
- **Palacio de Bellas Artes** - stunning Art Nouveau/Art Deco building
- **Templo Mayor Museum** - Aztec archaeological site and museum
- **Museo Soumaya** - free admission, impressive art collection
- **Museo Nacional de Arte (MUNAL)** - Mexican art from colonial to modern

### Historical Sites
- **Teotihuacán** - ancient pyramids (40 km outside city)
- **Xochimilco** - colorful trajineras (boats) on ancient canals
- **Basílica de Guadalupe** - important Catholic pilgrimage site
- **Palacio Nacional** - Diego Rivera murals depicting Mexican history
- **Templo Mayor** - ruins of the main Aztec temple

## Food & Dining

### Must-Try Dishes
- **Tacos al Pastor** - marinated pork with pineapple
- **Quesadillas** - filled tortillas (often without cheese here!)
- **Tamales** - corn masa wrapped in corn husks
- **Chilaquiles** - fried tortilla chips in salsa
- **Mole** - complex sauce with chocolate and chilies
- **Pozole** - hominy soup
- **Tlacoyos** - oval-shaped masa cakes
- **Elote** - grilled corn with toppings
- **Pan dulce** - sweet bread from local bakeries

### Where to Eat
- **Mercado de San Juan** - exotic ingredients and gourmet food
- **Mercado Roma** - upscale food hall
- **Street stands** - best tacos and quesadillas
- **Contramar** - famous for seafood
- **Pujol** - world-renowned fine dining (reservation required)
- **El Cardenal** - traditional Mexican breakfast

### Street Food
- Taco stands throughout the city (especially in evenings)
- Churros and tamales vendors
- Fresh fruit stands with chili powder
- Esquites (corn in a cup) carts
- Mercados for authentic local meals

## Getting Around

- **Metro**: Extensive, cheap (5 pesos), can be crowded
- **Metrobús**: Dedicated bus lanes, prepaid card required
- **Uber/Didi**: Convenient and safe ride-sharing
- **Turibus**: Hop-on-hop-off tourist bus
- **Ecobici**: Bike-sharing system
- **Peseros**: Shared minibuses (for adventurous travelers)

## Essential Travel Tips

- **Altitude**: Mexico City is at 7,350 feet (2,240m) - take it easy first day
- **Safety**: Use registered taxis or Uber, especially at night; avoid showing valuables
- **Water**: Drink bottled water only
- **Money**: Cash is widely used; many places accept cards
- **Tipping**: 10-15% at restaurants; small tips for various services
- **Language**: Spanish is primary; English limited outside tourist areas
- **Pollution**: Check air quality, some days can be hazy

## Cultural Experiences

- Attend a Lucha Libre wrestling match at Arena México
- Watch Ballet Folklórico at Palacio de Bellas Artes
- Visit on Day of the Dead (November 1-2) for colorful celebrations
- Explore art galleries in San Miguel Chapultepec
- Experience mariachi at Plaza Garibaldi (go with a group)
- Sunday bike rides on Paseo de la Reforma (car-free)

## Day Trips

- **Teotihuacán** - Pyramids of the Sun and Moon (1 hour)
- **Puebla** - Colonial city with Talavera pottery (2 hours)
- **Taxco** - Silver mining town in mountains (2.5 hours)
- **Cuernavaca** - "City of Eternal Spring" (1.5 hours)
- **Xochimilco** - Floating gardens and trajineras (within city limits)

## Best Markets

- **Mercado de la Ciudadela** - handicrafts and souvenirs
- **Mercado de Coyoacán** - local food and crafts
- **La Lagunilla** - antiques and vintage items (Sundays)
- **Mercado de San Juan** - gourmet ingredients
- **Mercado Roma** - modern food market

## Nightlife & Entertainment

- Bars and clubs in Roma and Condesa neighborhoods
- Mezcal and tequila bars throughout the city
- Rooftop bars with city views
- Live music venues and jazz clubs
- Zona Rosa for LGBTQ+ nightlife
- Polanco for upscale bars and lounges

## Where to Stay

- **Centro Histórico**: Budget-friendly, walkable to attractions, can be noisy
- **Roma/Condesa**: Trendy, great restaurants and nightlife
- **Polanco**: Upscale, safe, near museums
- **Coyoacán**: Quieter, artistic, residential feel
- **Zona Rosa**: Central, good metro access, diverse neighborhood

## What to Pack

- Comfortable walking shoes (lots of walking on uneven sidewalks)
- Light layers (temperature varies throughout day)
- Sunscreen and hat (strong UV at high altitude)
- Rain jacket (if visiting May-October)
- Reusable water bottle with filter
- Spanish phrasebook or translation app
