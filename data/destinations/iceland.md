# Iceland Travel Guide

Iceland is a land of fire and ice, offering stunning natural landscapes, from glaciers and volcanoes to geothermal hot springs and northern lights.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Summer (June-August)**: Midnight sun, all roads open, warmest weather
- **Winter (November-March)**: Northern lights, ice caves, winter activities
- **Shoulder Seasons (April-May, September-October)**: Fewer crowds, lower prices

## Top Attractions

### The Golden Circle
Classic day trip from Reykjavik:
- Thingvellir National Park (UNESCO site)
- Geysir geothermal area
- Gullfoss waterfall

### South Coast
Stunning waterfalls and black sand beaches:
- Seljalandsfoss (walk behind the waterfall)
- Skogafoss
- Reynisfjara black sand beach
- Vik village

### Jokulsarlon Glacier Lagoon
Floating icebergs and Diamond Beach

### Blue Lagoon
Famous geothermal spa near Reykjavik

### Reykjavik
Capital city attractions:
- Hallgrimskirkja church
- Harpa Concert Hall
- Old Harbor area
- Perlan Museum

## Essential Travel Tips

- **Rental Car**: Best way to explore Iceland at your own pace
- **Weather**: Unpredictable, dress in layers
- **Daylight**: Extreme variations (midnight sun in summer, limited daylight in winter)
- **Currency**: Icelandic Krona (ISK), cards widely accepted
- **Water**: Tap water is excellent and free

## Road Trip Tips

- Ring Road (Route 1) circles the island
- Watch for sheep on roads
- Check road conditions at road.is
- Many F-roads require 4WD and are closed in winter
- Gas up frequently, stations can be far apart
