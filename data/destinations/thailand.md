# Thailand Travel Guide

Thailand, known as the "Land of Smiles," offers tropical beaches, ornate temples, vibrant street food culture, and warm hospitality.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Cool Season (November-February)**: Best weather, peak tourist season
- **Hot Season (March-May)**: Very hot but fewer crowds
- **Rainy Season (June-October)**: Afternoon showers, lush scenery, best prices

## Top Destinations

### Bangkok
Bustling capital city:
- Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew
- Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)
- Chatuchak Weekend Market
- Floating markets
- Street food paradise

### Chiang Mai
Cultural hub in the north:
- Old City temples
- Night bazaar
- Elephant sanctuaries
- Doi Suthep temple
- Cooking classes

### Islands

#### Phuket
Largest island with diverse beaches:
- Patong Beach (nightlife)
- Kata and Karon (family-friendly)
- Old Phuket Town

#### Koh Samui
Palm-fringed beaches and luxury resorts

#### Krabi
Limestone cliffs and clear waters:
- Railay Beach (rock climbing)
- Phi Phi Islands
- Four Islands tour

### Ayutthaya
Ancient capital with temple ruins (UNESCO site)

## Essential Travel Tips

- **Visa**: Many nationalities get visa on arrival or visa exemption
- **Currency**: Thai Baht (THB)
- **Respect**: Dress modestly at temples, cover shoulders and knees
- **Bargaining**: Expected at markets (not in malls or fixed-price shops)
- **Scams**: Be aware of common tourist scams

## Must-Try Experiences

- Visit a floating market
- Get a traditional Thai massage
- Take a cooking class
- Ride a tuk-tuk
- Attend a Muay Thai match
- Visit an ethical elephant sanctuary

## Food Highlights

- Pad Thai
- Tom Yum Goong (spicy shrimp soup)
- Green curry
- Mango sticky rice
- Som Tam (papaya salad)
- Street food (safe and delicious)

## Packing Essentials

- Light, breathable clothing
- Modest clothes for temples
- Sunscreen and insect repellent
- Rain jacket (especially rainy season)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Beach gear

## Cultural Etiquette

- Remove shoes before entering homes and some shops
- Never touch someone's head
- Don't point feet at people or Buddha images
- Greet with a "wai" (prayer-like gesture)
- Respect the monarchy

## Budget Tips

- Street food is cheap and delicious
- Use local transportation (buses, songthaews)
- Book accommodation in advance for better rates
- Negotiate long-term stays at guesthouses
- Visit temples (mostly free or very cheap)
